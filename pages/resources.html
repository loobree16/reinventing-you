Resources

Books

Permission to Feel

By Marc Brackett Ph.D

Permission to Feel integrates psychological research, educational practice, and compelling stories, including Marc Brackett’s own life experiences, to make emotional intelligence come alive


Broke Millenial

by Erin Lowry

The book is aimed at 20-20 somethings who are dealing with both paying off debt and beginning to plan for the future.


Nutrition in Crisis

by Richard D. Feinman

Biochemist and medical researcher Dr. Richard Feinman explains the intricacies of nutrition and human metabolism in plain English. The book also offers an unsparing critique of the nutritional establishment, which continues to demonize fat and refute the benefits of low-carbohydrate and ketogenic diets―all despite decades of evidence to the contrary.

Podcasts

The Habit Coach with Ashdin Doctor

Host Ashdin Doctor covers topics like nutrition, fitness, health, sleep, and productivity. Each episode is centered around tips and motivation that encourage you to create new habits that impact your daily life.


Where Should We Begin

Host Esther Perel, a relationship therapist, tackles complex issues many of us face with our partner including sexlessness, infertility, communication problems, infidelity, trauma, regret, roles, and so much more.


TED Talks Health

Each episode is packed full of information from doctors and researchers on a truly wide range of topics. Whether you're curious about sleep, medication, vaping, or bad breath, this podcast has something for everyone.

Websites

CSPI

The Center for Science in the Public Interest is a science-based consumer advocacy organization for improving the food system to support healthy eating.


Active Minds

Active Minds is the nation’s premier nonprofit organization supporting mental health awareness and education for young adults.


Crisis Text Line

Crisis Text Line is free, 24/7 support for those in crisis. Text 741741 from anywhere in the US to text with a trained Crisis Counselor. Crisis Text Line trains volunteers to support people in crisis.

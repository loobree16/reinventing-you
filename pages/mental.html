Spiritual Health

This involves restoring spirtuality in yourself and in your life. Creating connections is also needed and encouraged when dealing with spiritual health. Connections helps to define a grounded presence in the world.

Types of Connections

  1. With a higher power.
  2. With yourself.
  3. With others.
  4. With nature

Spiritual health can be achieved through religious practices or implementing activities that help your mind and body feel at peace.

Questions to ask yourself when looking to boost spiritual health:

  1. What makes me feel most complete?
  2. When do I feel most connected with the rest of the world?
  3. Where do I find the most inner strength?
  4. What am I doing when I feel whole?

Activity suggestions to boost spiritual health

  1. Meditation (see meditation section below)
  2. Yoga
    1. Can help to lessen the strains on the body and mind.
    2. Taught at different levels so you don't have to be a yoga master to try it.
    3. Search for studios near you to see what options they provide.
  3. Traveling
    1. Doesn't have to be someplace far away.
    2. A chance to take a break and reflect on yourself, your experiences, and your surroundings.
  4. Volunteering
    1. There are always places looking for volunteers in your community.
  5. Mentoring
    1. This could also fall under volunteering.
    2. Helping others achieve dreams and goals with your knowledge and experience to help guide them.

Meditation

Meditation in Depth

What is Meditation?

“Meditation is a mind and body practice that has a long history of use for increasing calmness and physical relaxation, improving psychological balance, coping with illness, and enhancing overall health and well-being (NCCIH)”.

The goal of meditation is to feel inner peace and grounded in the present

There are different types of meditation:

  1. All meditation has the same goal, but they each combine different elements of calming, relaxing, and physical techniques.
  2. Click Here to learn moore about the different types of meditation.

Benefits

  1. Calms the mind.
  2. Gain a new perspective on stressful situations.
  3. Building new skills to manage your stress.
    1. Reduces negative emotions.
    2. Increases patience and tolerance to help assess the problems that are causing the stress.
  4. Increasing self-awareness.
    1. Grounding the mind to help focus on the present.
  5. Increasing imagination and creativity.
    1. Opens the mind to new ideas and helps to think clearly.

Nature and your Environment

How it affects you mental health

  1. The people that are in your environment can either improve or have negative effects on your mental health.
  2. The environment that you place yourself in affects your mood, mindset, and your living quality as a whole
  3. Not only your living space, but the outside environment as well
    1. Polluted air as well dirty environmental surroundings can cause negative effects on your mindset.

Best Practices

  1. Click Here
  2. Keeping your living space clean.
  3. Surround yourself with images, colors, and objects that bring you positive thoughts.
  4. Make sure you have a space where you can relax and relieve stress.
  5. Surround yourself with the right people that don't bring stress into your life (see Social Self Care).

Mental self-care also involves doing things that help you stay mentally healthy. Practicing self-compassion and acceptance, for example, helps you maintain a healthier inner dialogue.

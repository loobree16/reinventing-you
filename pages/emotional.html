Emotional Self-Care

What is Emotional Self-Care?

Activities that help you connect, process, and reflect on a full range of emotions.


Cultivating Emotional Intelligence

Emotional intelligence is ‘the ability to sense, understand, and effectively apply the power and acumen of emotions as a source of human energy, information, and influence.’ - Jack G. Montgomery of Western Kentucky University

In simpler terms, The more we understand our own emotions, the more we see these emotions in others, and then the more we can appropriately express our own.


Daniel Goleman, author of Emotional Intelligence, breaks down these skills into parts...


Self-Awareness

In certain situations, try to notice what you are feeling and why. Do you know what you’re good at, and what you’re not? Self-awareness means really knowing who you are, and being able to examine your own feelings and reactions.


Self-Management

Defined as the awareness and management of how one conducts themselves and interacts with the world. It’s your awareness of and how you express your emotions.

When a strong emotion arises, like anger, one should be able to recognize it, analyze its cause, assess its validity, and find ways to express it appropriately.


Self-Motivation

Self-motivation is the force that drives you to do things, to work towards your goals, as well as your own self-development. When you feel low on self-motivation, ask yourself why? Also, ask yourself what the costs to you are if you do not self-motivate. The cost could be your self-esteem, disappointing your peers, and poor grades or evaluations.

Your remedy could be many things, like seeing a therapist, taking medication, or planning your schedule in advance.


Empathy

Empathy is also vital to creating emotional intelligence within yourself.

Empathy allows you to understand other’s feelings and the effects your actions can have on others.

Boundaries

Boundaries are rules or limits that one creates for themselves for what is appropriate behavior for the people around them, as well as how to respond when someone breaks those rules.

It’s important to set personal boundaries for yourself so others know what you’re comfortable with and how to treat you. Boundaries help you to:

  1. Practice self-care and self-respect.
  2. Communicate your needs in your relationships.
  3. Set healthy limits in your relationships.
  4. Set physical boundaries, which include your body, sense of personal space, and privacy.
  5. Set emotional boundaries, which protects your self-esteem and ability to separate your feelings from others.

You may feel you cannot enforce boundaries for yourself for many reasons. It could be a fear of rejection, confrontation, or guilt. You could also have just never been taught healthy boundaries.

If you’re in a situation with someone who is physically dangerous or threatening, you should work with a counselor, or therapist to help get you to safety, and boundary setting may be a part of this.


Self-Compassion

Self-compassion refers to relating to one’s self with kindness.

It means being kind to ourselves in good times and bad, not judging yourself harshly for our perceived shortcomings. Many of us fall into the trap of self-criticism, which is a self-defeating tendency. Self-compassion leads to resilience. It involves facing our failures or mistakes in a completely different way, with kindness.


  1. Being kind and understandings oneself as one would a friend. We're intrinsically deserving of care and concern just like everybody else.
  2. Understanding we are not alone in our mistakes and weaknesses. It's actually instrinsic to being human, and a normal part of everyone's life.
  3. Being mindful of your emotions and feelings without over-identifying with them. Validating our emotions without adding fuel to the fire.

People who have self-compassion also have greater emotional intelligence, social skills, and overall happiness.

Practice mindfulness, and be aware of your thoughts and feelings. More specifically, your inner critic. What is it trying to protect you from? Find compassion for these feelings and by extension, yourself.

Emotional self care is a process in which we bring our awareness to our emotions and take the steps necessary in order to honor and care for these emotions. This is essential for our wellbeing.

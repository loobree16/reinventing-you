Social Self-Care

Social Media

When to stop scrolling

Check your accounts; know what you want and stop when you don't see it.

  1. Does the content bring you joy, or any other beneficial emotion?
  2. Does the content add value to your life?

When you are on social media many times throughout the day and you find Yourself having a hard time focusing its time to disconnect and learn to focus In your real life again.

How to absorb content online.

The digital age has given us access to a wide scope of information at our fingertips. With such easy access to so much information we have started to have a shorter span of focus due to the tendency of going through a plethora of content, which is a lot for our brain to absorb and filter. Reading has become harder especially on screens that can be harsh on the eyes if used for a long time. Staying in one source and fully reading and giving your attention to one thing at a time is better than “power browsing” and skimming, making you work harder to really focus.

Communication Tips

Setting Boundaries

How communication boundaries work

  1. In our external system: Boundaries protect our bodies and environment someone breaking your boundaries would be through physical intrusion or line crossing. Ex. someone standing too close to you or snooping through your belongings.
  2. In our internal system: Boundaries protect our thoughts, feelings and actions. We can be violated by aggressive tones in voice or slander and demanding behavior towards us.

Weak Boundaries

  1. When your boundaries are weak you have low self esteem and you close yourself off to others and have no trust or open communication.

Strong Boundaries

  1. You have strong relationships when you have strong boundaries. You grow internally and you protect yourself against being violated by others' negative behaviors or negative communication.

10 steps to having Extensive Communication boundaries:

  1. 1. Educate people in advance: tell them about your boundaries.
  2. 2. Ask the other person to stop: tell them when they cross a line.
  3. 3. Demand that the other person stop.
  4. 4. Walk away.
  5. 5. Raise your standards: Tell them what you do in this situation.
  6. 6. Warn the other person when they are getting too close.
  7. 7. Upgrade who you associate with.
  8. 8. Extend the boundary: you can change what you tolerate.
  9. 9. Anticipate and prevent.
  10. 10. Announce the boundary.

Understanding Perspectives

  1. Try to understand why the other person thinks they are right, what is their reasoning?
  2. Ask the person why they believe what they do.
  3. Listen to understand, not disprove or discredit.
  4. Accept differences.

Time Management

  1. Put all your to do items in one place, like a master to-do list.
  2. As you analyze your list Do, Defer, Delegate, Delete.
  3. Do short tasks first
  4. Defer harder tasks for later when the small stuff is done.
  5. Delegate things to others when possible in group tasks or chores.
  6. Delete invaluable tasks that have been on the list a while, or you don't feel like doing.
  7. Assign tasks priority numbers or due dates.
  8. Focus on the most important tasks for most of your time.
  9. Focus on one thing at a time.
  10. Use a priorty matrix:
Urgent Not Urgent
Important Quadrant I: Urgent and Important Quadrant II: Not Urgent, but Important
Not Important Quadrant III: Urgent, but Not Important Quadrant IV: Not Urgent, Not Important

Embracing Alone Time

  1. Add a peaceful habit to your daily routine.
  2. Disconnected from devices during alone time.
  3. Don't be a silence filler, allow yourself.
  4. Make alone time enjoyable.
  5. Make your bedtime a ritual place.
  6. Take a walk and immerse yourself in the surroundings.

Manage Romantic Relationships

How to spot red flags:

  1. Lack of communication.
  2. Irresponsibility, immaturity, and unpredictability.
  3. Lack of trust.
  4. Significant family and friends don't like your partner.
  5. Controlling behavior.
  6. Feeling insecure in the relationship.
  7. A dark or secretive past.
  8. Non-resolution of past relationships.
  9. The relationship is built on the need to feel needed.
  10. Abusive behavior.

Finding toxic traits within yourself

  1. You withhold affection and/or compliments.
    1. You wait until your partner is exactly how you manipulate them into being, or you use affection to get what you want.
  2. You're passive aggressive.
    1. You intentionally play with your partner's feelings, including trying to make them jelous.
  3. You intentionally play with your partner's feelings, including trying to make them jelous.
    1. Your foundation should be solid, manipulation and tests are unnescessary and toxic.

Manage Platonic Relationships

Tips on Communication

  1. Smile - have a friendly demeanor.
  2. Make a first great first impression - use the person's name in conversation, speak clearly.
  3. Engage in good conversation - use direct eye contact, pay attention, keep your body language open, give feedback, ask questions, show you are listening.

How to Make Friends Online

  1. Join study groups.
  2. Attend your hobby city events.
  3. Join a community you are interested in online.
  4. Connect on social media.

How to Stay Connected during the Pandemic

Social connection is a human need. Continuing meaningful relationships is part of maintaining good mental health. Here are nine ways to stay connected during quarantine and social distancing.

  1. Call someone and have an authentic conversation. Call a friend a family member or a significant other. Share thoughts and memories and enjoy their virtual company.
  2. Connect with friends and family on social media. Interact with ideas and opinions and spark up conversation.
  3. Create or join a virtual book club or discussion group. Or join a live chat for a live stream video. Join any virtual community that interests you.
  4. Join a friend or family member to exercise with you (6 feet apart of course).
  5. Sit on your porch and chat with people that cross by or neighbors, converse in a friendly way of course, don’t heckle the lawn steppers. Enjoy the activity in your neighborhood. Be sure to social distance as well.
  6. If you have a dog take them to a dog park or go even if you don’t have one. Walk and give out kind short conversations to park goers at a safe distance.
  7. Volunteer on outreach calls or with social distancing and physical protection gear in person to be there for those who need someone to connect to.
  8. Join group activities in your community at a safe distance such as singing from balconies with apartment mates or light exercise in your building corridor.
  9. Check on neighbors once in a while especially ones that live alone and ask if they need any assistance with chores such as grocery shopping or if they need anyone to talk to.

Social self-care involves doing things that nurtures and deepens the relationships with people in your life.
